# Open Content Plan

This is the reset plan for making the product musically credible without depending on made-up tabs.

## Recommendation

Build the app around:

- public-domain songs and arrangements
- Creative Commons `CC BY` / `CC BY-SA` guitar studies and miniatures
- a small number of simple connector exercises only where needed

Do not use:

- `CC BY-NC`, `CC BY-NC-SA`, or `CC BY-NC-ND` if the product may be commercial
- `CC BY-ND` if we need to adapt the material into our format
- random scraped tab pages unless the site and the specific score explicitly allow reuse

## Why This Is Safer

Verified source rules:

- Mutopia says all music on the site may be downloaded, copied, distributed, modified, performed, and recorded.
- Mutopia says `CC BY-SA` and `CC BY` allow commercial use.
- Creative Commons says `CC BY` and `CC BY-SA` allow commercial reuse, while `NC` does not.
- The U.S. Copyright Office says works published in the United States before January 1, 1930 are in the public domain.

Primary sources:

- Mutopia license details: <https://www.mutopiaproject.org/legal.html>
- Creative Commons license guide: <https://creativecommons.org/share-your-work/cclicenses/>
- U.S. Copyright Office Circular 15A: <https://www.copyright.gov/circs/circ15a.pdf>

## Best Audience

The strongest audience is still:

- late beginners
- returning players
- self-taught players who want recognizable material and visible progress

The product pitch should be:

`Learn guitar through familiar melodies, simple riffs, and short classical guitar pieces that actually feel good to play.`

That is clearer than:

- pure scale trainer
- fake-song riff generator
- advanced-player lead lab

## Recommended Product Shape

Keep 3 worlds, 8 levels each.

Use this content mix:

- World 1: easiest recognitions + easiest guitar movement
- World 2: familiar songs with more weight and clearer rhythm
- World 3: real guitar miniatures and stronger melodic pieces

Target ratio:

- 4 familiar-song levels per world
- 3 guitar-study / miniature levels per world
- 1 boss level per world

## Proposed 3-World Curriculum

### World 1: First Songs

Goal:
- instant recognition
- simple rhythm
- low frustration

Suggested lineup:

1. Twinkle melody
2. Carcassi Prelude (very simplified excerpt)
3. Ode to Joy
4. Good King Wenceslas
5. Carcassi Prelude 2
6. Amazing Grace
7. Silent Night
8. Boss: First Songs Medley

### World 2: Song Weight

Goal:
- make songs feel fuller on guitar
- add root notes and stronger rhythm

Suggested lineup:

1. Ode to Joy with root support
2. Amazing Grace with bass anchors
3. Greensleeves simplified
4. Ah Vous Dirai-Je Maman variation excerpt
5. Carcassi Sonatina No. 1 excerpt
6. Auld Lang Syne guitar version
7. Silent Night with simple accompaniment notes
8. Boss: Song Weight Suite

### World 3: Guitar Pieces

Goal:
- shift from melody-recognition into real guitar identity

Suggested lineup:

1. Carcassi Sonatina No. 1
2. Carcassi Sonatina No. 2 excerpt
3. Greensleeves guitar version
4. Auld Lang Syne guitar version, harder arrangement
5. Carcassi Study No. 17 excerpt
6. Carcassi Prelude in minor
7. Sonatina / study hybrid boss prep
8. Boss: Stage Piece

## Product Direction

The best commercial direction is now:

- guided path
- familiar songs
- short classical guitar pieces
- optional account sync
- daily practice and progress features

This gives the app a real identity:

`a modern guitar practice game for late beginners, built around legally reusable recognizable material`

## Backup Pivot

If content sourcing still feels too heavy, the backup pivot is:

- keep the UI
- stop selling it as a song product
- make it a practice companion

That version focuses on:

- pitch tracking
- rhythm follow-along
- setup calibration
- streaks
- daily drills
- user-imported tabs or MIDI later
